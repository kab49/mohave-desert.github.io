Suliformes

The order Suliformes is a group of birds that includes pelicans, cormorants, anhingas, frigatebirds, and boobies, among others. These birds are adapted for life in the water and are generally large in size, with long wings and webbed feet. They are carnivorous and feed on a variety of prey, including fish, squid, and crustaceans. Suliformes are found in a variety of habitats all around the world, and are known for their elaborate courtship displays. There are many different species of birds in the order Suliformes; cormorants, anhingas, frigatebirds, and boobies.
